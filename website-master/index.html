Services



  • Hatha Yoga

    Moving your body slowly and deliberately into different poses that challenge your strength and flexibility, while at the same time focusing on relaxation and mindfulness.

  • Trauma Informed Yoga

    There are many theories about trauma and how or even where it’s stored in the body. In trauma-informed yoga, we aim to move sensations that have been activated by PTSD from one spot, where the trauma is present, to another spot in the body, where the trauma is not.

  • Yin Yoga

    While “yang” yoga focuses on your muscles, yin yoga targets your deep connective tissues, like your fascia, ligaments, joints, and bones. It’s slower and more meditative, giving you space to turn inward and tune into both your mind and the physical sensations of your body.

  • Restorative Yoga

    Restorative yoga is a style of yoga that encourages physical, mental, and emotional relaxation. Appropriate for all levels, restorative yoga is practiced at a slow pace, focusing on long holds, stillness, and deep breathing.

  • Prenatal Yoga

    Much like other types of childbirth-preparation classes, prenatal yoga is a multifaceted approach to exercise that encourages stretching, mental centering and focused breathing.

  • Postnatal Yoga

    A modified, low-intensity yoga practice. This type of yoga is designed to help your body recover. Postnatal yoga offers the most benefits during the first three months after childbirth.

  • Pranayama

    Pranayama is an ancient practice of yogic breathing techniques used to harness and regulate universal life force energy known as prana. It is an important practice under the umbrella of yoga, often integrated into asana (physical postures) practice as well as a preparatory step for meditation

  • Subtle Bodies

    Perfect for anyone looking to deepen their energetic practices through the lens of yoga philosophy, the kosha bodies, the chakras, and the human aura.

  • Reiki

My History with Yoga

My history with yoga does not begin with myself: it begins with my dad. In 2006, my dad was experiencing crippling depression. Medication stopped working for him and his good friend, Marilyn, suggested practicing yoga. The changes I observed were astounding and I could not deny that yoga was something special.

While I had acknowledged these positive changes in my dad, the concept of yoga, or, what I thought the concept of yoga was, was laughable. I had been an athlete my whole life and am naturally a very extreme person, so focusing on breathing and relaxation was not in my repertoire. It wasn’t until my depression became unmanageable, in 2017, that I decided to give yoga a genuine chance.

I was not practicing yoga to practice yoga; I was doing asanas solely to see what kind of crazy poses I could get into. I was doing yoga to show off my flexibility and drive. My depression lessened because it felt good to show off, to contort my body into shapes that not very many of my friends could.

I dove in completely, thinking of nothing else but yoga and how I could apply each new thing I learned in my everyday life. Each lecture brought more clarity, each chapter brought more enlightenment, each practice brought more love. I embraced yoga as a lifestyle and my depression seemed like a distant memory. It was then that I truly understood yoga and yogic philosophy. It was then that I felt like a yogi.

Why I teach Yoga

I teach because movement of the body is critically linked to your health. It gives us healthy joints, good circulation, concentration and even mental well-being. The breath brings oxygen into the body, directly affecting the amount of energy that is released to your cells. We do these two things every day without any special notice, but if we set an intention and harness the energy it takes to move the body or breathe, we can melt away stress, improve our ability to face each day with love and happiness, and even access our higher selves.

I am now able to utilize yoga in a healing way. Everything you need resides within. It is my goal as an instructor to guide you to channel that energy to live happier, healthier lives.

Yoga has done for me what nothing else has: it has allowed me to repair damaged relationships, to lower my stress and depression, to accept myself as the divine being I am, to connect with energy that surrounds us, and to feel real, authentic love. Even the thought of getting on my mat gives me goosebumps. Yoga is pure, yoga is kind, yoga is fulfillment.
